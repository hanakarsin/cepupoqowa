A Kenyan woman based in the United States (US) has pleaded guilty to defrauding men over KSh 430 million in romance scams.

Florence Mwende Musau, 36, pleaded guilty to the conspiracy to commit wire and bank fraud charges on Monday, June 21, alongside five others and her sentencing was scheduled for October 14, 2021.

According to a statement from the United States Department of Justice, the Kenyan national was involved in a string of romance frauds where she adopted fake online identities to gain victim's trust and affection with the intention of defrauding them.

"To carry out the schemes, Musau used fake passports in the names of numerous aliases to open bank accounts in and around Boston to collect and launder the proceeds of the romance scams," the statement indicated.

Museu would then execute large cash withdrawals from the accounts, often multiple times a day, generally less amounts to evade detection and currency transaction reporting requirements.

Museu is now staring at a 30-year jail term, five extra years of supervised release and a fine of up to KSh 107 million.

"Whichever is greater, restitution, and forfeiture. Sentences are imposed by a federal district court judge based upon the U.S. Sentencing Guidelines and other statutory factors," the statement indicated .

What are romance scams?

Romance scams occur when a criminal adopts a fake online identity to gain a victim’s affection and trust.

The scammer then uses the illusion of a romantic or close relationship to manipulate and steal from the targets.

According to Ameriprise, criminals often target emotionally vulnerable individuals, like someone who lost a partner.

The scams usually take place via social media or online dating sites, and the scammer is rarely available to meet in person.

After getting emotionally close to their victims, the perpetrators often start asking for money using believable lies like medical expenses, travel expenses and other emergencies.
