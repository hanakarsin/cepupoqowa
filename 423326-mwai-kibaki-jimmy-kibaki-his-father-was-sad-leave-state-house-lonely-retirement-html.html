Mwai Kibaki: Jimmy Kibaki Says His Father Was Sad to Leave State House, Lonely after Retirement

 Posted on February 29, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  457 words  |  Chauncey Koziol
  • Jimmy Kibaki said former president Mwai Kibaki was sad to leave state house because he felt he had not accomplished all his goals
  • The wealthy businessman said the transition from being a president to a retired head of state was challenging and daunting for Mwai Kibaki
  • The situation was further aggravated by Mama Lucy Kibaki's death which left him to face life after retirement all alone

Former president Mwai Kibaki's son Jimmy Kibaki has opened up on the state of his father almost a decade after he went into retirement after the completion of his presidential tenure.

Mwai Kibaki took the helms of the country as Kenya's third president in 2002, and after the lapse of his two five-year presidential terms, he went into retirement in 2013 after handing the tools of power to the incumbent President Uhuru Kenyatta.

In a candid interview with the Daily Nation, Jimmy recalled that when it was time for Kenya's third president to leave state house, there was a cloud of sadness hovering over him as he felt he had not executed all the projects he had set out to do for Kenyans.

The 58-year-old reckoned the transition from being a president, an incredibly busy job where every second of the job-holder is planned, to moving to a place where your phone almost never rings is daunting, and sometimes lonely.

"There was sadness when Mzee (Kibaki) left state house because he did not feel as if he had accomplished all that he had set out to do. You will be surprised how short 10 years can be," he said.

Kibaki's retirement, coupled with the death of Mama Lucy Kibaki, his wife, even made the situation lonelier, according to Jimmy who narrated that the 89-year-old fell ill when she died.

The wealthy businessman said his father's sickness was majorly caused by grief as he and the former first lady had a very close relationship.

Uhuru's retirement

Jimmy advised President Uhuru Kenyatta to plan well for his retirement as the transition phase can be challenging and needs a lot of adjustments.

"I hope he (Uhuru) has planned his retirement well because being a president is a job that ends suddenly," he said.

Jimmy's remarks came just less than a year to Uhuru's retirement in 2022 when he will also complete his presidential terms.

Already, several politicians have expressed interest in succeeding the head of state; they include Deputy President William Ruto, Musalia Mudavadi, Raila Odinga, Kalonzo Musyoka, and Gideon Moi.
