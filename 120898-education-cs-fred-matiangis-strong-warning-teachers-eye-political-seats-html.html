Resign and focus on campaigns, Fred Matiangi tell teachers

 Posted on June 29, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  398 words  |  Chauncey Koziol

- Education Cabinet Secretary Fred Matiangi has asked teachers who are interested in political seats in the 2017 general election to resign in order for Teachers Service Commission to get their replacements

- Matiangi said that the teachers spend most of their time campaigning at funerals instead of being in class hence affecting academic performance of their students 

Education Cabinet Secretary Fred Matiangi has told teachers targeting political seats in the 2017 general election to resign and focus on campaigns.

Fred Matiangi, on Wednesday, April 20, said that he already had a list of teachers who spend time campaigning instead of being in class.

According to the Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission calendar, civil servants interested in public office have up to February 9, 2017 to resign.

Teachers had once suggested that they be allowed to vie for the posts and resume teaching if they are defeated in the polls.

Fred Matiangi said that schools cannot post good results when teachers are ever at funerals trying to convince their constituents.

Teachers' absenteeism was a big problem that had to be handled candidly, Fred Maitiangi said.

Their absenteeism, especially in public schools, is indeed one of the greatest challenges facing the education sub-sector.

Various government and non-governmental reports have illustrated this problem, which has been the sole major cause of poor show of public schools during the national exams.

According to one report that was prepared by the World Bank titled The Education and Health Services in Kenya – Data for Results and Accountability in 2013, a fifth of schools surveyed had a teacher absence rate of between 20% and 40%, and for a tenth of schools it was 40%.

But the most shocking revelation by that report was teachers who are present at school but absent from class.

For every 100 public school teachers, only 55 were in class were teaching and 27 were at school but not teaching. This translates to an average teaching time of only two hours and 40 minutes in public schools out of a five hours and 40 minutes official teaching day.
