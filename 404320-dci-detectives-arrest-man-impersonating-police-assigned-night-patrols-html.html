Directorate of Criminal Investigation (DCI) sleuths based in Lang'ata have arrested a man who has been impersonating police officers and conducting self-assigned night patrols.

The suspect, Gregory Simiyu Mabele, was donning fatigues belonging to the Administration Police, that is the jungle green camouflage sweater and side pocket pants, but his sneakers sold him out.

In a statement shared on Twitter on Saturday, February 27, the investigations agency said they arrested Simiyu past curfew hours walking around Nairobi's Southlands pretending to be offering public safety and security services.

Detectives suspect the 40-year-old was part of a wider cabal of criminals, who pose as police officers and betray the trust of unsuspecting members of the public, by defrauding and stealing from them.

Simiyu will be arraigned in court on Monday, March 1, to answer to charges of being in unlawful possession of government property contrary to Section 324 (2) of the Constitution.

He will also be charged with impersonating a police officer contrary to Section 101 (1) (b) of the National Police Service Act.

Joshua Waiganjo

In a related story, Joshua Waiganjo was cleared of impersonation and abuse of office charges.

Waiganjo was charged alongside former Rift Valley provincial police boss John M’mbijiwe and former Anti-stock Theft Unit in-charge Remi Ngugi, now deceased.

Waiganjo faced impersonation charges while M’mbijiwe and Ngugi faced abuse charges.

However, Principal Magistrate Joe Omido in a ruling delivered via video link from Kwale Law Courts to Nakuru said the prosecution failed to prove Waiganjo was an impersonator.
