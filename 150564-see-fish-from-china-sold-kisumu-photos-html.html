Kisumu residents defy MPs' directive on fish from China

 Posted on January 1, 0001  |  2 minutes  |  339 words  |  Chauncey Koziol

- Kisumu residents go against their MPs' call to boycott fish imported from China

– Chinese firm – Zhangzou Longyi Food Company Limited – has been selling imported tilapia in Kisumu for the last three months

– The imported tilapia goes for as little as KSh 30 compared to locally sourced ones that are sold for at least KSh 100

Kisumu fish traders have defied their lawmakers's directive to boycott tilapia fish imported from China.

A number of MPs from Kisumu county and the Lake Victoria region had, on Tuesday, July 5, urged the locals to boycott the fish as imports would kill their fishing industry.

Others in the joint statement were: Olago Aluoch (Kisumu Town West), Shakeel Shabbir (Kisumu East), James Nyikal (Seme), Joseph Ndiege (Suna West), Gideon Ochanda (Bondo), Millie Odhiambo (Mbita) and John Kobado (Uriri).

Despite the statement, Kisumu residents have continued to go for the fish from China as they find them way cheaper than the locally sourced fish.

The Standard, on Monday, July 4, surprised many when it reported that a firm was selling imported fish in Kisumu due to shortage from local suppliers.

The imported tilapia is packaged, frozen and imported to Kenya by Zhangzou Longyi Food Company Limited with EASF being its local agent, the report read.

According to the paper, a piece of the imported tilapia fish goes for KSh 30 in the market. A price much lower than the local tilapia which cost between KSh 100 and KSh 200.

Director of State Department of Fisheries in Kisumu, Robert Wanyama, said he was aware of EASF’s activities.
