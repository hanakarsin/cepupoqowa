Kibra by-election: Raila endorses Imran Okoth in front of Mariga

 Posted on June 21, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  375 words  |  Hettie Henneman

The race for Kibra seat got murkier for Jubilee candidate McDonald Mariga on Friday, September 27, after Opposition leader Raila Odinga endorsed his fiery opponent, Imran Okoth, at a public event attended by both.

Raila who led mourners in a requiem mass for the 8 pupils who died following Precious Talent School tragedy held at Telcom Grounds near Ngong Road, did not let the chance to drum up support for the ODM candidate to slip.

In his usual charismatic style, the former premier declared before the crowd and in front of the Jubilee candidate that Kibra was his home turf and the ODM candidate was the people's choice.

" As you all know Kibra is my house and I have walk with my son Imran, stand up and come in front for people to see you," Raila said amid cheers from the crowd

He then held the left hand of his candidate and asked residents to elect him as the Kibra member of parliament in a by-election slated for November 7.

"Mbunge wa Kibra nini nanii? Nanii? Imran Okoth sawa sawa? Asanteni sana. (Who is the Kibra MP? Who? Imran Okoth okay? Thank you very much," he said in move some observers noted was meant to intimidate Mariga

As the clock continues to tick towards the D-Day for the contest, accusations and counter-accusations emanating from the growing political activities in the urban constituency continue to ensue

The race which was initially seen as an easy ride for the Orange party has now turned out to be a complicated contest particularly after President Uhuru Kenyatta endorsed Mariga as the Jubilee candidate.

The Raila-led outfit was also weakened after coalition partners under the National Super Alliance (NASA), FORD Kenya and Amani National Congress (ANC), opted to field candidates for the race.
