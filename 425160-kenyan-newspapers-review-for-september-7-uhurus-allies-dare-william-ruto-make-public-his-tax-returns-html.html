Kenyan Newspapers Review For September 7: Uhurus Allies Dare William Ruto to Make Public His Tax

 Posted on July 16, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  589 words  |  Lourie Helzer

On the Tuesday of September 7, Kenyan dailies focused on the action by the ruling Jubilee Party challenging Deputy President William Ruto to make public his taxes following his admission that he owns 70% of the properties recently listed by Interior Cabinet Secretary Fred Matiang’i.

The dailies also reported that former nominated senator Paul Njoroge had filed a lawsuit to extend President Uhuru Kenyatta’s term by three months.

1.Daily Nation

The daily covered widely the ongoing onslaught against Ruto, who is now being targeted by Uhuru’s allies over his source of wealth.

On Monday, September 6, Uhuru’s close political confidants led by Jubilee secretary-general Raphael Tuju and the party’s parliamentary leadership launched a scathing attack against the DP.

Tuju said that the only thing left for Ruto to do was publicly reveal his tax returns to the Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA).

2.The Standard

The publication reported that former nominated senator Paul Njoroge filed a petition at the High Court to bar the Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) from conducting the 2022 General Election.

Njoroge argued that conducting the polls slated for August 9, 2022, will be unconstitutional.

He said that Uhuru and his deputy Ruto assumed office on November 28, 2017, after the repeat presidential election held on October 26, 2017.

IEBC chairperson Wafula Chebukati had on August 8, 2021, announced that the 2022 polls would be held on August 9, 2022, and issued a schedule of procurement of key materials, equipment and personnel.

3.Taifa Leo

As the clock ticks towards the 2022 General Election, the Swahili publication reported that dirty politics is once again rearing its ugly head.

According to the daily, politicians use force to divide Kenyans among tribal lines while publicly engaging in insults.

While speaking on Sunday, September 5 at Hardy P.A.G church, Makadara MP George Aladwa engaged in tribal politics, but Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) leader did not stop him.

Kapseret MP Oscar Sudi, who is facing several cases in court relating to hate speech, recorded a short video clip last week insulting Matiang’i.

4.The Star

The Star reported that counties that host nomadic pastoralists would prioritise the distribution of the COVID-19 Johnson and Johnson vaccines.

This is one of the government's actions to ensure the 10 million December target is attained.

Kenya is home to many nomadic pastoralists, especially in the arid and semi-arid lands in the northern part of the county in areas such as Isiolo and Marsabit.

The targeted communities include Turkana, Pokot, Samburu, Endorois, Tugen, Sabaot, Somali and Borana.

5.People Daily

The publication reported that the government had banned all roadblocks and static police checks across the country.

This comes on the backdrop of reports that some rogue officers were using such points to extort bribes.

National Police Service (NPS) headquarters also directed all complaints by motorists to be investigated promptly for appropriate action to be taken against the traffic police officers.

Following a meeting by Inspector General Hillary Mutyambai, Deputy Inspector General of Police Edward Mbugua and Traffic commandant Mary Omari, it was noted that despite other measures having been put in place, corruption was still rampant, especially among the traffic officers.
