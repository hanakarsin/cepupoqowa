List of 15 best international companies in Kenya in 2020

 Posted on April 4, 2024  |  7 minutes  |  1324 words  |  Chauncey Koziol

Some of the world’s largest multinational corporations have set their regional headquarters in Kenya over the past few years. With their African or East African operations centered in Kenya, the country’s business outlook is greatly enhanced. Coincidentally, they also turn out to be some of the best companies in Kenya. That is either in terms of service of delivery through efficient management or in terms of top companies to work for and fetch good salaries.

The registrar of companies Kenya through the state law office has a long list of this multinational corporations. Most of them are engaged in different sectors of the economy. While some are the world’s top technology giants, others are engaged in industrial parts manufacturing and many others in assembling various machines to cater for their markets.

Chinese company in Kenya, for instance, have dominated the construction industry. That is ranging from the construction of roads to skyscrapers and pretty much any mega project.

List of international companies in Kenya

With slightly a competitive edge in terms of securing financing and heavy construction equipment, one can see why they have had to dominate the scene. The following is a list of international companies in Kenya. You may want to note these are just a few of the many available in the Kenya business list.

  • ASUS the Asian technology giant focuses on the larger East African region.
  • BASF that operates from Westlands Nairobi and focuses its activities on the entire sub-Saharan Africa region.
  • Bank of China that focuses on financing Chinese companies engaged in large-scale construction projects within the East African region.
  • Bharti Airtel that offers mobile phone communication services and other telecommunication services in several African countries.
  • Blackberry LTD. The giant technology devices manufacturer focuses on the east and central African market.
  • Cisco System. Headquartered in Nairobi and focusing on the whole of Africa.
  • CGTN. China’s premier television channel has its African segment headquartered in Nairobi.
  • Coca-Cola. The American beverage manufacturer’s African segment is based in Upperhill Nairobi.
  • General Electric. The company operates from Westlands Nairobi and has its eyes on the whole African market.
  • Heineken. An international premium beer manufacturer focusing on the African and the larger Indian Ocean neighborhood from High Ridge, Nairobi.
  • IBM. The world premiere manufacturer of various machines serves the African market from its Nairobi office.
  • Toyota. A Japanese car manufacturer that is focused on the assembling of various motor vehicles for its African market. It operates from its Mombasa Road, Nairobi office.
  • Standard Chartered Bank. The multinational banking institution is focused on the African region and runs its activities from its Westlands, Nairobi office.
  • Mitsubishi. The Japanese heavy equipment and earth movers’ manufacturer also operates its Africa division from the city.
  • Nestle. Their Nairobi beverage hub focuses on the east, west, and central African regions.

    The list of companies is by no means exhaustive neither is it prepared in the order of their profit-making capability. The Kenya business directory will most likely get you more of the top tier international companies operating in the country, but it suffices to say these are definitely on top of the league.

    Most companies incorporated in Europe, for example, are public companies with shares listed in some of the popular capital markets around Europe. So are the Japanese companies like Toyota whose shares are listed on the Tokyo stock exchange. On the hand, most of the Chinese business in Kenya is run by companies wholly owned by the Chinese state. This is true for companies like CGTN, China-Africa Development Fund, Xinhua, and the Bank of China.

    In their areas of operation, the Chinese companies have a business model that quickly see them emerge as the topmost performers in their respective field. The strategy includes ready funding from Chinese banks and sometimes direct contracts resulting from the government to government negotiations between the Kenyan and Chinese governments.

    On the other hand, most of the companies outside the arrangement sometimes have to jostle for market share with some of the best private companies in Kenya. With enhanced efficiency and streamlined management structures with little squabbles, however, the international companies do not take long to grow in the market.

    In its niche, for example, Coca-Cola maintains a near market monopoly as it maintains a widespread grip on the beverages market in Kenya. With a huge financial muscle and experience of many years, the company has created a competitive edge. Mostly this is through intensive and extensive advertisement campaigns. Colored television ads, well-worded radio ads, catchy billboards, and well-timed roadshows throughout the country.

    Another aspect of their campaigns is their involvement in local activities like sponsoring a football tournament, sponsoring local leagues, and other community-based competitions. Unless a competitor builds a financial war chest, it will be difficult to get some market share off Coca-Cola.

    But it is not always so easy for multinational corporations sometimes local companies have taken roots, and they fight back fiercely against competitors. Bharti Airtel for example despite its international experience across the globe has found it hard to penetrate the Safaricom dominated telecommunications sector. Some companies like YU just had to give up and leave the market despite their international appeal.

    Others in the telecommunication industry like orange with investments from France are still struggling to penetrate the market. The Majority local owned Safaricom has packaged itself in a manner that it locks in users. Be it as it may, it is possible for us to still reach a conclusion that the companies with an international touch are better managed and offer pleasant experiences to customers and employees alike.

    Some analysts have contended that the good performance of international companies over their local counterparts in a similar industry is the prior research that they do before entering a market, a well-funded entry, and the scope of operations. The scope of operation of most international companies located in the country is beyond the country itself. Most focus on regional opportunities. Some keep their focus on the larger eastern Africa region, others include the central African region, and indeed many others have their focus on a continental scale of operations. This somewhat ensures that they don’t have to intensely focus on the small market in one country.

    In some industries, the international companies have to outdo one another. Some areas like motor vehicle assembling do not really have a local counterpart. The competition is between companies from Europe and the Asian giants. It is dominated by companies like Toyota, Mitsubishi, General Motors, Volkswagen, and other companies incorporated in Kenya such as AVA, KVA, DT Dobie but assembling for companies like Hyundai and Volkswagen. The market scope is also pretty much the same for the companies.

    The intense competition between equals with similar financial status and market focus gives rise to enhancements in management and operational efficiency. The international focus also ensures that company management in the country of incorporation is more keen on costs and revenues since they will soon or later have to explain the performance or profitability of a venture in a particular market. This helps to keep divisional managers on their toes leading to superior performance as compared with the laxity we at times witness in the management of locally incorporated companies.
