For many years, a 60-year-old farmer with a visual impairment from the Volta region in Ghana, Lawrence Demebu, vowed never to beg to survive.

With the support of a white cane, he walks for over 30 minutes, carefully navigating his farm daily to work long hours to put food on his table.

My Joy Online reported that Demebu had a dream to build a decent house for himself, but he could not afford it, and in 2020, hehe appealed to kind-hearted people to help him build a retirement home.

Inside his mud house was a small mattress made with a sack filled with straw. Unfortunately, the upper part of the room wall has developed a crack that could collapse anytime.

Philanthropist builds house

The good news is that after his inspiring story was aired, a philanthropist surprised the 61-year-old farmer with a decent house replacing his weak mud house.

A grateful Demebu was lost of words to express his happiness, so he burst into a song and dance before he could find the right words to say.

"In the past, where I was living was so depreciated. The roof was leaking, and the walls had so many cracks," he said."Fortunately today, by his grace, I am entering a new house that is why I am happy," added a grateful Demebu.

The house was build after the CEO of KCS Susan Akojo, and her friends were touched by Demebu's story, and without thinking twice, she mobilised funds to build him a decent one-bedroom house.

It was indeed a happy ending for Demebu.

Demebu worried due to lack of family

However, his inability to find a wife and start a family leaves him worried about his old age.

At 60 years, his biggest fear is who will take care of him when he is old and frail.

“I cook, wash, clean and also fetch water for myself, but who will take care of me when I am old," he posed.

In 2017, the Afadjato South District awarded him the Best Disability farmer.

His prize was a pair of wellington boots, two cutlasses, a knapsack sprayer, a fridge and a radio set. He also received GH¢800 (KSh 14,540) to support his farming.
