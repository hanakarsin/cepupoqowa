Raila should be accorded enough security when he jets back - ex-Uhuru political adviser

 Posted on March 14, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  365 words  |  Chauncey Koziol

- President Uhuru Kenyatta's former political adviser has urged the government to accord Raila Odinga enough security when he jets back to the country

- Cherangany MP Joshua Kutuny said Raila should be given security and escort from the airport to the rally NASA is expected to hold

Raila Odinga is due back in Kenya on Friday, November 17, after a four-day visit in the United State of America where he went to push for electoral reforms in Kenya.

Cherengany Member of Parliament, Joshua Kutuny (Jubilee Party), called on the police to provide adequate escort to the opposition leader upon his return until his final location at Uhuru Park grounds for a grand rally.

According to KTN News, Kutuny said the police should not give any excuses of why the opposition cannot use the grounds like they've done in the past.

"I want to urge the police, when Baba arrives, he should be given enough security. He should be given proper escort all the way to where they will be having the rally. The issue of police saying they will not allow them Uhuru Park should not arise. They should be given that opportunity, let them ventilate," said Kutuny.

Kutuny is President Uhuru Kenyatta former political adviser

According to prior TUKO.co.ke reports, the National Super Alliance team called on supporters to come out in their hundreds of thousands to welcome Baba at the Jomo Kenyatta International Airport.

While in America, Raila called on Western leaders to rethink their support for the Jubilee regime.

Raila and his opposition allies said they neither recognised the 'sham' October 26 repeat presidential election nor President Uhuru's victory because the election had allegedly been predetermined in Uhuru's favour.

This was one of the reasons Raila gave when he withdrew his candidature from the election.
