Mombasa: KDF soldier takes own life after killing colleague under unclear circumstances

 Posted on May 4, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  393 words  |  Hettie Henneman

- Police said Kibet was on leave but sneaked into Mtongwe barracks and turned rogue

- He snatched a rifle from Kimtai and shot him three times before turning the gun on himself

- Cases of suicide among soldiers and police are increasing at an alarming rate

A Kenya Navy soldier has committed suicide shortly after shooting his colleague to death.

TUKO.co.ke has learned that the soldier, identified as Kibet was on leave and had visited Kenya Navy barracks in Mtongwe before he turned rogue.

The serviceman snatched a G3 riffle from a colleague identified as Kimtai and shot him three times before turning the rifle on himself.

"Kibet who has just arrived on pass leave , secretively sneaked into the barracks where he found Senior private Kimtai actively engaged on duty. He snatched his G3 rifle and shot him thrice fatally injuring him before turning the gun to himself, the police report read.

Colleagues who responded to the incident, rushed Kibet at Kenya Navy sick bay but he succumbed to his injuries.

The bodies have since been moved to Coast General Hospital mortuary pending postmortem.

Detectives have launched investigations to unearth the motive behind the bizarre incident.

Cases of police officers and soldiers killing their colleagues have been rife in the country.

Officers and soldiers have also been hit hard by cases of suicide dogging their career.

This comes barely a month after another KDF soldier killed himself after shooting three of his close relatives to death in Eldoret.

In a separate story, TUKO.co.ke also reported on an incident where a junior officer ended life of his senior in Samburu.

To reduce cases of suicide, Inspector General of Police Hillary Mutyambai recently urged senior officers to listen to their juniors.
