Meet the Somali woman locked in a BITTER fight with Jubilee senator over child

 Posted on May 30, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  364 words  |  Chauncey Koziol

- Fatuma Mohamed wants Narok Senator Stephen Ole Ntutu to take care of their 13 year-old son whom he has ignored

- Fatuma has now claimed that the senator and his family chased her away from Narok as she had gone to ask for her rights

The Somali woman involved in a bitter fight with a Jubilee Senator over child upkeep, has now revealed more about their stormy battle.

Fatuma says she was kicked out of a house together with her child for failure to pay rent and upon calling Ntutu he ignored her pleas for help.

Fatuma said her son named Jamal Ole Ntutu is 13 years-old but in class five due to years of neglect and lack of school.

Fatuma said she had recently gone to Narok to demand for her rights but was chased away by the senator and his family.

Fatuma fighting tears said she and her son could go days without food as the senator never sends her upkeep apart from school fees which he pays directly to school bank account.

The matter was presented before court in 2013, where she was compelled to have a DNA test to prove he was his son.

"The documents enclosed disclose that the court did order DNA tests at the Government chemists when the results returned positive proof that the honorable senator is indeed the minor's biological father," reads a part of the document.

Fatuma is now calling on the government, FIDA and politicians to help her get her rights.
