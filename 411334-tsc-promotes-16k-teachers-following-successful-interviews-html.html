At least 16,152 have been promoted to various positions ahead of Term Three, which commences on Monday, May 10.

The TSC chief executive officer Nancy Macharia said the commission picked successful tutors from a pool of other applicants following the interviews done in December and February 2021

In a press statement on Friday, May 7, Macharia said the interviews were part of the final phase of the Collective Bargaining Agreement signed between teachers and the government.

"The commission will communicate to all teachers who were interviewed through the existing communication channels," she stated, cautioning teachers against fraudsters claiming they can influence the outcome of the interviews.

The commission had job openings for deputy principals, headteachers, deputy headteachers, senior lecturers, and curriculum support officers, among others.

Those seeking to be promoted to the deputy principal grade D3 were required to have served in the grade of deputy principal grade D2 for at least three years.

Back to school

In a separate but related development, Education Cabinet Secretary George Magoha confirmed KSh 7.5 billion had been released to secondary schools ahead of the reopening.

"We, therefore, hope that schools will be adequately prepared to put in place measures to adhere to the Ministry of Health COVID-19 protocols," he said during the closure of KCSE exam marking on Friday, May 7.

At the same time, Magoha also revealed that the announcement of the KCSE results would be made in due course.

"All I can tell you is that the results will come out reasonably well as expected so that if we require, for example, more of our children to apply to teachers training colleges to be trained on the Competency-based Curriculum (CBC), they should benefit," he added.
