Nandi Hills residents compel MP Alfred Keter to convey Ruto's, not Uhuru's message on extra-judicial

 Posted on June 9, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  561 words  |  Almeda Bohannan

- Keter was in Lessos to address issues of police brutality and calm down charged locals

- The MP told residents he had a message from President Uhuru Kenyatta and wanted to pass it to them

- He was, however, cut short by dissenting voices that demanded him to deliver Deputy President William Ruto's message instead

Nandi Hills MP Alfred Keter had a difficult time passing President Uhuru Kenyatta's message of condolence to Lessos residents after two people were shot and killed by police officers.

The area MP who was accompanied by governors Jackson Mandago (Uasin Gishu), Stephen Sang (Nandi) were in Lessos on Friday, June 26, to address locals angered by the acts of police officers that led to two deaths earlier n Wednesday, June 24.

When Keter rose to speak, he began informing the crowd that he and leaders present in the meeting had been sent by Uhuru.

He expounded that the Head of State had been perturbed by the police officers' actions and would ensure justice is administered.

"The reason we have top officers here is that they have been sent by our president. He is concerned and disturbed about what happened to the people of Lessos and he is sent me his condolences," said Keter before he was cut short by locals who wanted to know whose message he was conveying.

"Who sent you?", Posed one resident before the whole crowd started to shout making it difficult for the legislator to express himself.

"It is the president that has sent me," Keter repeated while calling on the locals to calm down so that he could explain more.

His sentiments were also drowned with deafening shouts from the crowd who demanded to know what deputy president William Ruto said instead.

"Tell us about Ruto. We do not want to hear the rest," loud voices were heard from the crowd.

After sensing growing dissenting voices, Keter promised the crowd that he would also share what Ruto had said about the happening.

"Listen to how our discussion with the president went then I will also talk about Ruto. You think you know Ruto more than I do? Furthermore, he is my good friend," said the two-term legislator, a move that saw the crowd convert their jeers to thunderous laughter.

Earlier, his Kapseret counterpart Oscar Sudi had called on the locals to stand firm and cautioned police officers against using excessive force when discharging their mandate.

The demonstrations that led to the death of two more locals were as a result of an earlier fatal shooting of a cobbler living with a disability.

It is believed that the cobbler was shot by a police officer after he attempted to quell a quarrel between the law enforcer and a Boda Boda rider who he had arrested for not putting on a mask.
